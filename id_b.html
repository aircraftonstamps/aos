Avro Tudor

Aircraft regn is G-AHNK, named "Star Lion". It was operated by British South American Airways (BSAA).

This aircraft was the first of 6 Tudor IV aircraft to be delivered to BSAA, in September 1947. It was operated by BSAA for flights between London and Nassau, Bermuda, and the Azores. There isn't any info on what happened to this particular aircraft - it seems to have operated as both passenger and freighter aircraft until the early 1950s.

Avro 691 Lancastrian

Aircraft regn is G-AGWH, named "Star Dust". It was manufactured by A V Roe & Co, c/n 1280, for British South American Airways (BSAA).

The G-AGWH, named "Star Dust" was the second of 18 Mk III Lancastrians built specifically for the BSAA. It first flew on 11th November 1945, and was in operations with BSAA from early 1946.

On 2nd August 1947, this aircraft was operating the Buenos Aires to Santiago route on a flight from London when it crashed into Mount Tupungato in the Argentine Andes, killing all aboard. The wreckage was not discovered until 1998 because of the location of the crash.
